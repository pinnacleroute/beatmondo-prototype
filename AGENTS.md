# Prototype Instructions

Run the local server yourself and open the preview in the in-app browser. Do not give the user server-start instructions when you can run it.

Before making substantial visual changes, use the Product Design plugin's `get-context` skill when the visual source is unclear or no longer matches the current goal. When the user gives durable prototype-specific design feedback, preferences, or decisions, record them in `AGENTS.md`.

When implementing from a selected generated mock, treat that image as the source of truth for layout, component anatomy, density, spacing, color, typography, visible content, and hierarchy.

Durable beatmondo direction: always write the brand as lowercase "beatmondo" in visible UI and docs, honoring Gary Burke's original brand style. Treat the product as a premium, gated, rights-controlled sync licensing ecosystem - private club + premium catalog + licensing workflow + secure delivery + catalog intelligence - for serious music supervisors, brands, studios, agencies, strategic partners, artists, rightsholders, and global sync opportunities. Keep public browsing selective; full metadata, project tools, licensing workflows, protected WAV masters, stems, and downloads belong to approved/paid buyer tiers. Use Gary Burke red as a strategic brand accent inside a dark cinematic luxury system, with muted brass/gold only as secondary support.

Durable access direction: represent three buyer tiers - Discovery Access, Professional Buyer, and VIP Sync Access. VIP Sync Access should feel meaningfully faster and more curated, with private selections, priority review, pre-approved terms where available, concierge support, fast-track licensing, and premium secure delivery. Simulate authentication, payment, secure links, file delivery, AI insights, and rights document workflows with frontend state and mock data unless explicitly asked to build real backend behavior.

Durable terminology direction: use "Explore Music" for buyer-facing discovery and reserve "Catalog" for admin/internal operations. Use "Request License" for track/project licensing CTAs and "Request Access" only for account/workspace access. Keep availability, workflow status, delivery state, and admin review state distinct; use "Quote Required" for track availability and "Quote Needed" for internal pipeline status. Prefer "protected master audio" in explanatory copy and "WAV master" only when naming the delivered file. Use "Editorial Hub" for the overview content area, with "Stories", "Media Episodes", "Short Sync Clips", "Catalog Highlights", and "VIP Picks" as child/content labels instead of Blog/Podcast naming.

Preserve enough inset around the reframed video for the completed icon, wordmark, tagline, and glow to remain fully visible; use silent looping playback and the static logo as the reduced-motion and playback-failure fallback.

Durable homepage transition direction: transition directly from the full-bleed video hero into the beige "beatmondo standard" section; do not leave a dark brown divider, border, or exposed legacy background seam between them.

Durable homepage hero headline: on desktop, distribute "Curated Music.", "Protected Rights.", and "Premium Access." across a single screen-wide row close to the bottom edge of the hero, aligned left, center, and right. Stack the three phrases on mobile for legibility.

Durable account direction: provide buyer-facing Sign Up / Request Access, Log In, Forgot Password, and social sign-in entry points as polished simulated frontend flows. Keep authentication and social login behavior mocked unless a real backend is explicitly requested.

Durable password gate direction: use a gold primary button on the password preview page, not Gary Burke red.

Durable interaction direction: commercial use-case tiles should pop up on hover with a polished lift, slight scale, stronger depth, and a restrained gold border highlight; preserve keyboard focus and reduced-motion behavior.

Durable commercial use-case direction: keep the tiles free of visible "Explore Music" labels; the full tile remains the interactive discovery target.

Durable button motion direction: translate the supplied `Without music.mp4` brand animation into a continuous 1.8-second VIP action loop with no settle phase: waveform amplitude keeps moving, acoustic rings repeat, and a gold light pass travels continuously across the button. Give gold primary buttons a single highlight sweep and gentle lift, outline buttons a quiet border/fill transition, text actions an underline reveal, and icon buttons a compact lift/press response. Keep labels stationary, support hover, keyboard focus, and touch press for the supporting transitions, and remove waveform travel, ripple, sheen, glow, lift, and scaling under reduced-motion preferences.

Durable non-VIP button motion direction: keep non-VIP motion short and interaction-triggered. Request License actions receive a restrained shield glow; Explore Music actions move their ArrowRight icon 4px; play controls emit one sound ring; save controls use a compact fill/pulse confirmation; public navigation reveals a red-to-gold underline; active private navigation uses a vertical gold/red indicator; loading submit buttons show a moving progress line and success states receive a quiet green confirmation glow. Disabled buttons must remain still and destructive actions should use color/border transitions only.

Durable editorial collections direction: use Gary Burke red for the section-level Explore Music CTA and the collection actions; make every View collection action bold and high-contrast.

Durable mini-player direction: treat the fixed player as a compact protected-preview control, not a trimming editor. Show elapsed time and preview duration around a labelled Protected Preview rail, animate one clear playhead continuously during playback, and use a restrained allowed-range tint behind progress. Do not show draggable-looking handles unless trimming is genuinely interactive. Keep Details secondary, maintain strong keyboard focus, expose progress semantics, preserve a 42–44px play/pause target, and keep waveform texture static with reduced-motion support.

Durable mini-player dismissal direction: provide both minimize and close controls. Minimize preserves playback and progress while collapsing the player into a compact full-width bar with play/pause, artwork, title, timing, progress ring, restore, and close actions. Close stops playback, resets progress, and removes the player without confirmation; selecting a track afterward reopens the full player.

Durable mini-player layout direction: keep both expanded and minimized playback states fixed edge-to-edge across the full viewport at every screen size, including over the sidebar. Use a flush bottom transport-bar treatment with no floating outer margins or rounded container corners. Keep controls minimal: play/pause, artwork and track identity, timing/progress, minimize or restore, and close; do not include Details in the fixed player.

Durable player tracker visual direction: use a restrained signal rail rather than repeated full-height waveform stripes. Keep the unplayed rail near-black with a faint gold center line, show progress with a quiet Gary Burke red-to-muted-brass gradient, and use one thin ivory-gold playhead with a small cap and subtle glow. Keep the rail around 30–32px high with a faint inner highlight; do not add shimmer, pulsing, fabricated waveform peaks, or decorative motion beyond actual playback progress.

Durable featured-track direction: keep The SMYRK's “The End of Jason Todd” pinned first in every general or eligible track list, including catalog results regardless of selected sort. Preserve semantic subsets such as saved tracks and artist-specific tracks, but place it first whenever it belongs in that subset.

Durable preview synchronization direction: use the real audio element as the source of truth for play, pause, completion, error, and progress state. All card, detail, artist, admin, expanded-player, and minimized-player controls for the active track must reflect the same playback state, preserve elapsed time across pause/resume and minimize/restore, restart after completion, and reset only when the player is closed.

Durable audio compatibility and timing direction: serve protected previews through a broadly supported WAV primary source with a compressed AAC fallback. Whenever the protected preview is shorter than the full recording, label the two runtimes explicitly; playback controls and list duration columns should show preview duration, while metadata may separately show full track duration.

Durable preview seeking direction: make the expanded protected-preview signal rail a genuine seek control limited to the approved preview. Support click/tap seeking, continuous pointer dragging, and keyboard arrow movement; update elapsed time and the playhead immediately without changing the current play/pause state. Keep the minimized player compact and require expansion for scrubbing.

Durable seek-focus visual direction: do not draw a focus outline or highlighted border across the full preview rail after click, tap, drag, or keyboard focus. Keep focus feedback localized to the active playhead through a restrained width/glow change.

Durable The SMYRK direction: treat the supplied “The End of Jason Todd” WAV as protected source-master material and expose only a derived browser preview. Present The SMYRK through the supplied studio and live photography, connect the artist profile to Explore Music, Track Detail, protected preview, Request License, and rights review, and do not invent publishing, ownership splits, personnel, PRO registration, or licensing eligibility. Durable analytics direction: centralize prototype reporting across existing browser-persisted source records, exclude unauthorized records before aggregation, and keep internal, buyer-organization, and artist scopes distinct. Always separate licensing finance from membership simulations; distinguish quoted, contracted, invoiced, collected, refunded, outstanding, and simulated weighted-pipeline values. Label projections, demand scores, engagement indicators, cohort analysis, fixed currency conversions, and incomplete attribution clearly, and retain accessible tables alongside charts.

Durable privacy direction: treat compliance and privacy as legally cautious, browser-persisted governance simulations, never as automatic GDPR, CCPA, UK GDPR, Indian privacy-law, or other certification. Keep optional consent explicit, versioned, withdrawable, and separate from contractual, financial, rights, security, delivery, and audit processing. Exclude third-party, legal, security, payment-credential, raw-signature, pricing-logic, token, storage-path, and approval-note data from user exports; apply permissions before discovery and aggregation. Deletion must be reviewed against active contracts, licences, invoices, rights history, disputes, security evidence, audit retention, and legal holds, using anonymization, restriction, detachment, or retention where appropriate.

Durable audit direction: treat centralized audit logging as a normalized, append-only evidence layer across every prototype module. Redact credentials, MFA values, secret-bearing URLs, protected storage references, raw payment data, and signature images before persistence; keep buyer/artist/organization visibility separate from finance, rights, legal, security, and super-admin evidence. Apply permission filtering before counts, analytics, and exports, deliver generated exports through temporary access, preserve correlation and before/after context, and clearly label browser-based integrity, retention, legal hold, and immutability behavior as simulated rather than certified or tamper-proof.

Durable email direction: centralize transactional email and in-app communication around versioned approved templates, safe recipient resolvers, permission-filtered history, stable references, deduplication, preferences, quiet hours, scheduling, retries, and cancellation. Never claim real delivery, expose reusable private tokens, place internal rights/legal/risk detail in external messages, or reveal BCC/internal routing to buyers and artists. Required security, payment, signature, verification, licence, suspension, revocation, and delivery notices override optional opt-outs; optional reminders respect user preferences and quiet hours. Preserve rendered historical versions, connect safe actions to authenticated pages or fresh expiring access, and prevent email/audit recursion.

Durable admin-permissions direction: treat authorization as deny-by-default policy evidence combining active account state, versioned role inheritance, scoped assignments, direct grants, explicit denials, temporary elevations, delegations, approval limits, separation of duties, conflicts, and impersonation restrictions. Explicit denial overrides every ordinary grant, temporary access expires automatically, service actions revalidate independently of route and navigation visibility, and field-level restrictions return understandable redacted placeholders. External buyers and artists never inherit internal access; critical roles, overrides, emergency access, restricted exports, and impersonation require reasons, audit evidence, and secondary approval where designated. Preserve the true administrator during view-only impersonation and block signing, payment, protected download, verification, and other legally significant actions.

Durable licence-generation direction: generate every licence from immutable accepted-quote, executed-contract, rights, buyer-eligibility, and licensing-payment versions. Keep the licence record, generated document, delivery authorization, and secure delivery package separate. Never authorize protected master audio delivery before an issued active licence and separate delivery authorization; never treat a quote, signature, deposit, or track visibility alone as issuance. Preserve historical issued records and source snapshots rather than retroactively rewriting them when current upstream state changes. All licence documents, approvals, legal-state changes, and delivery controls remain clearly simulated and not legally certified.

Durable secure-delivery direction: keep delivery authorization, package, package version, asset entry, entitlement, session, manifest, and event as separate linked records. Lock exact asset versions on activation and never silently update an active package when storage assets change. Buyer access must be temporary, organization- and user-scoped, revocable, count-limited, and revalidated against the issued licence, payment, rights, verification, membership, authorization, package, asset, and entitlement at both session creation and completion. Never expose private storage references or imply real encrypted transfer; preserve completed events and historical versions after replacement, suspension, expiry, or revocation.

Durable expiring-access direction: route every protected stream, download, document, upload, and signature entry through a central temporary-access record before creating the underlying scoped session. Persist only mock token-hash placeholders and safe suffixes in long-term history; show a full mock token only at initial generation or exchange, never in buyer lists or audit tables. Keep storage references, expiring access, user-facing temporary URLs, tokens, entitlements, sessions, attempts, and protected resources separate. Revalidate scope, action, user, organization, session/device simulation, use limits, verification, membership, licence, delivery, payment, rights, and asset state at exchange and protected action; expiry or refresh must never silently restore an inactive entitlement.

Durable quote-calculation direction: present all calculated pricing as fictional internal guidance, suggested ranges, or draft review-subject amounts—never definitive market pricing. Keep calculations versioned and explainable, require rights-aware licensing eligibility and role-based internal approvals before sending, and keep buyer acceptance distinct from contract, payment, tax, invoice, rights transfer, or protected delivery. Preserve buyer-safe quote views that hide internal assumptions and notes. Never calculate or expose pricing for The SMYRK while licensing eligibility remains unconfirmed.

Durable contract and e-signature direction: keep accepted quotes, contract drafts, signed agreements, countersignatures, effective agreements, generated licences, payment, and delivery authorization as separate linked states. Present every contract, legal review, consent, signature, certificate, and signed-document record as a clearly simulated prototype artifact—not legal advice, a binding agreement, identity verification, or a certified e-signature-provider result. Contracts must preserve the exact quote, rights, template, clause, and contract versions used; buyers may see only their organization’s buyer-safe documents and comments. Never authorize payment, licence generation, protected master audio, stems, or delivery merely because a quote was accepted or one signer completed.

Durable licensing-payment direction: keep platform-membership billing, licensing invoices, contract obligations, payment attempts, bank-transfer reconciliation, account credits, refunds, receipts, licence generation, and delivery authorization as separate linked ledgers and states. Present every card, bank, authentication, refund, reconciliation, receipt, tax, and financial record as a clearly simulated prototype artifact; never request or persist real full card numbers, CVCs, bank credentials, or processor secrets. A successful licensing payment may satisfy only its allocated obligation and must never broaden signed usage, create a licence automatically, or unlock protected master audio, stems, or delivery without all remaining contract, rights, licence, and delivery conditions.

Durable file storage and streaming direction: model public media, protected previews, private masters, private stems, rights documents, commercial documents, temporary processing, and archives as separate logical storage classes with one central access-policy decision. Preview playback must use short-lived simulated sessions, never expose private storage references or imply delivery, and stop when access expires or is revoked. Keep replacement versions non-current until simulated processing completes, preserve superseded versions and audit activity, quarantine integrity mismatches, scope artist file visibility to the artist's own organization, and scope buyer commercial documents to the related organization. Clearly label all storage, scanning, transcoding, watermarking, signed-access, and CDN behavior as frontend simulation rather than real infrastructure.

Durable watermarked-preview direction: resolve preview variants in this order—valid buyer-specific, collection-specific, VIP, Professional, Discovery, Public, then unavailable—while always enforcing rights, publication, buyer verification, membership, organization, expiry, and session rules. Keep source audio untouched and private; every generated output is a distinct `PROTECTED_PREVIEW` record, streaming-only, with visible simulated spoken/tonal cues and clearly labelled simulated forensic references. Use safe organization phrases only, never personal emails or phone numbers. Policy or source changes should create regeneration work without silently overwriting approved versions, and revocation or expiry must immediately stop active sessions. Artists may review and request corrections only for their own previews; buyer-specific sessions and internal policy configuration remain hidden from them.

Durable membership billing direction: keep membership, buyer verification, licensing fees, and delivery approval separate. Membership controls platform access and service level; it never includes track licence fees, never grants VIP verification, and never independently unlocks protected master audio, WAV masters, stems, or pre-approved terms. Derive effective buyer access centrally from account state, verification, active membership and billing status, restrictions, delivery approvals, and explicit administrator overrides. Keep all payment, invoice, tax, refund, retry, enterprise, and card-authentication behavior clearly labelled as simulated frontend state until a secure production backend and compliant providers are explicitly requested.

Durable rights database direction: treat rights records as structured, versioned, human-reviewed operational evidence rather than proof of legal ownership. Keep master, composition, publishing, contributor, sample, contract, territory, restriction, approval, document, dispute, review, audit, and licensing-eligibility states distinct. Buyer and artist surfaces must expose only role-safe summaries; contacts, ownership evidence, contracts, internal rationale, legal notes, risk analysis, and other parties' data remain restricted. Rights blockers must override membership and delivery access, and historical licence records must remain linked to the rights version used at approval.

Durable search infrastructure direction: route catalog discovery, suggestions, facets, counts, collections, recommendations, saved searches, and similar-track results through one visibility and relevance service. Never calculate buyer-facing suggestions or facet counts from records the current account cannot access. Exact title and artist relevance must outrank popularity, while rights eligibility, verified membership entitlements, buyer verification, organization-specific curation, and artist ownership determine visibility. Treat project-derived attributes as removable search preferences and label all natural-language interpretation as deterministic prototype parsing rather than AI.

Durable track-ingestion direction: keep source tracks, ingestion records, asset versions, rights records, and search documents linked but operationally separate. Upload never implies technical approval, rights verification, search eligibility, or publication. Preserve superseded and rejected asset versions internally, keep artist-visible messages deliberate, and never expose internal notes, failed files, or unpublished metadata to buyers. Publication must derive from technical, metadata, artwork/lyrics, catalog, rights, search, revision, processing, and final-approval readiness; unpublishing and archiving must preserve existing licence, rights, asset, and audit history. All scanning, extraction, waveform, transcoding, watermark, storage, and delivery states remain clearly simulated until backed by production services.
